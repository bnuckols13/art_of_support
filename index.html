🤝 Understanding Support

Click each card to discover key insights about giving support

💬 Perfect Words Myth

Support isn't about having the perfect words or solutions. Often, the most powerful support comes from simply being present and acknowledging someone's experience. "I see you" can be more healing than "Here's what you should do."

🧘 Presence Over Advice

Sometimes presence is more powerful than advice. Sitting with someone in their pain, offering your calm energy, and simply listening can provide more comfort than trying to solve their problems.

🎯 Different Support Types

People need different types of support: emotional (listening, validation), informational (practical help, resources), or appraisal (gentle challenge, perspective). The key is asking what they need.

💪 Receiving is a Skill

Receiving support takes courage and practice. It means being vulnerable, letting others see our struggles, and trusting that we're worthy of care. This is just as important as learning to give support.

🎭 Real-World Scenarios

Choose the most supportive response

😰
Scenario 1: Your friend is overwhelmed
"I'm feeling so overwhelmed with everything. I can't seem to get anything right, and I feel like I'm falling behind everyone else."
"You just need to organize your time better. Have you tried making a schedule?"
This jumps straight to problem-solving without acknowledging their feelings. While practical, it might make them feel unheard.
"That sounds really hard. I can hear how much you're struggling right now. What feels most overwhelming?"
Perfect! This acknowledges their feelings, shows you're listening, and invites them to share more without immediately trying to fix things.
"Everyone feels that way sometimes. You'll get through it!"
While well-intentioned, this minimizes their experience and offers false reassurance without really connecting with their struggle.

👂 Listening Support

What it looks like: Giving your full attention, reflecting what you hear, asking open questions.

When to use: When someone needs to process emotions or feel heard.

Example: "Tell me more about that... It sounds like you're feeling really frustrated about the situation."

✅ Validation Support

What it looks like: Acknowledging their feelings as valid and understandable.

When to use: When someone is doubting themselves or feeling shame.

Example: "Your feelings make complete sense given what you're going through. Anyone would feel overwhelmed in this situation."

🛠️ Practical Help

What it looks like: Offering specific, concrete assistance or resources.

When to use: When someone has identified a specific need or problem to solve.

Example: "Would it help if I researched some therapists in your area?" or "I can bring dinner over this week."

🎯 Gentle Challenge

What it looks like: Offering a different perspective with care and timing.

When to use: When someone is stuck in negative patterns and open to feedback.

Example: "I hear you saying you always mess up, but I'm remembering how well you handled the situation last month..."
